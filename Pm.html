Pm
Promethium
Atomic Number: 61

Atomic Mass: ~145 (no stable isotopes)

Electronegativity: 1.13

Density: ~7.26 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1315 K

Boiling Point: 3273 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f⁵ 6s²

Discovery

Promethium was discovered in 1945 by Jacob A. Marinsky, Lawrence E. Glendenin, and Charles D. Coryell at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, USA.

Isotopes

Promethium has no stable isotopes. The most common is ¹⁴⁵Pm with a half-life of 17.7 years.

Uses

- Nuclear batteries
- Luminous paint (historical)
- Research in atomic energy
- Portable X-ray sources

Fun Fact

Promethium is named after the Titan Prometheus, who stole fire from the gods—symbolizing the element’s radioactive “fire.”
