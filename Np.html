Np
Neptunium
Atomic Number: 93

Atomic Mass: 237

Electronegativity: 1.36

Density: 20.45 g/cm³

Melting Point: 917 K (644 °C)

Boiling Point: 4175 K (3902 °C)

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f4 6d1 7s2

Discovery

Neptunium was discovered in 1940 by Edwin McMillan and Philip H. Abelson. It was named after the planet Neptune, following Uranium in the periodic table.

Isotopes

Neptunium has no stable isotopes. The most important is Np‑237, with a half‑life of 2.14 million years.

Uses

- Breeding plutonium in nuclear reactors
- Research in nuclear physics
- Potential use in nuclear batteries
- Limited applications due to radioactivity

Fun Fact

Neptunium was the first transuranic element discovered, marking the start of man‑made elements beyond uranium.
