K
Potassium
Atomic Number: 19

Atomic Mass: 39.0983

Electronegativity: 0.82

Density: 0.862 g/cm³

Melting Point: 336.53 K (63.38 °C)

Boiling Point: 1032 K (759 °C)

Electron Config: [Ar] 4s¹

Discovery

Potassium was first isolated in 1807 by Sir Humphry Davy using electrolysis of molten potash (KOH).

Isotopes

Potassium has three naturally occurring isotopes: Potassium-39 (³⁹K), Potassium-40 (⁴⁰K), and Potassium-41 (⁴¹K).

Uses

- Fertilizers (potash)
- Potassium hydroxide in soaps and detergents
- Fireworks (violet flame color)
- Biological importance in nerve function and muscle contraction

Fun Fact

Potassium reacts explosively with water, producing hydrogen gas and a strong alkaline solution.
