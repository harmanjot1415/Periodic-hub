Hg
Mercury
Atomic Number: 80

Atomic Mass: 200.59

Electronegativity: 2.00

Density: 13.53 g/cm³

Melting Point: 234 K (-39 °C)

Boiling Point: 630 K (357 °C)

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f14 5d10 6s2

Discovery

Mercury has been known since ancient times. Its name comes from the Roman god Mercury, and its symbol Hg derives from the Latin "hydrargyrum", meaning "liquid silver".

Isotopes

Mercury has several stable isotopes, including Hg‑196, Hg‑198, Hg‑199, Hg‑200, Hg‑201, and Hg‑202.

Uses

- Thermometers and barometers
- Fluorescent lamps
- Dental amalgams
- Gold and silver extraction
- Scientific instruments

Fun Fact

Mercury is the only metal that is liquid at room temperature, making it unique among elements.
