U
Uranium
Atomic Number: 92

Atomic Mass: 238.03

Electronegativity: 1.38

Density: 18.95 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1405 K (1132 °C)

Boiling Point: 4404 K (4131 °C)

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f3 6d1 7s2

Discovery

Uranium was discovered in 1789 by Martin Heinrich Klaproth. It was named after the planet Uranus, discovered just eight years earlier.

Isotopes

Uranium has two main isotopes: U‑235 (fissile, used in nuclear reactors and weapons) and U‑238 (fertile, used to breed plutonium).

Uses

- Nuclear fuel for power plants
- Nuclear weapons
- Radiation shielding
- Dating rocks (uranium‑lead method)

Fun Fact

Uranium‑235 is the isotope that powered the first nuclear chain reaction in 1942.
