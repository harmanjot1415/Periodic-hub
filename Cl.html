Cl
Chlorine
Atomic Number: 17

Atomic Mass: 35.45

Electronegativity: 3.16

Density: 3.214 g/L

Melting Point: 171.6 K (-101.5 °C)

Boiling Point: 239.11 K (-34.04 °C)

Electron Config: [Ne] 3s² 3p⁵

Discovery

Chlorine was discovered in 1774 by Carl Wilhelm Scheele, and later confirmed as an element by Sir Humphry Davy in 1810.

Isotopes

Chlorine has two stable isotopes: Chlorine-35 (³⁵Cl) and Chlorine-37 (³⁷Cl).

Uses

- Water purification and disinfection
- Production of PVC plastics
- Bleaching agents
- Pharmaceuticals and antiseptics
- Chemical industry (chlorides and chlorinated compounds)

Fun Fact

Chlorine gas was used as one of the first chemical weapons in World War I, but today it’s vital for clean water.
