Ca
Calcium
Atomic Number: 20

Atomic Mass: 40.078

Electronegativity: 1.00

Density: 1.55 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1115 K (842 °C)

Boiling Point: 1757 K (1484 °C)

Electron Config: [Ar] 4s²

Discovery

Calcium was first isolated in 1808 by Sir Humphry Davy using electrolysis of lime and mercuric oxide.

Isotopes

Calcium has six stable isotopes, the most abundant being Calcium-40 (⁴⁰Ca).

Uses

- Cement and concrete production
- Metallurgy (reducing agent)
- Nutritional supplements
- Essential for bones and teeth
- Alloys and chemical industry

Fun Fact

Calcium is the most abundant metal in the human body, vital for muscle contraction and nerve signaling.
