Lr
Lawrencium
Atomic Number: 103

Atomic Mass: 262

Electronegativity: ~1.3 (estimated)

Density: ~14.4 g/cm³ (estimated)

Melting Point: ~1900 K (estimated)

Boiling Point: unknown (radioactive decay dominates)

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f14 7s2 7p1

Discovery

Lawrencium was discovered in 1961 by a team at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. It was named in honor of Ernest O. Lawrence, inventor of the cyclotron.

Isotopes

Lawrencium has no stable isotopes. The most notable is Lr‑262, with a half‑life of about 4 hours.

Uses

- Purely scientific research
- Studies of heavy element chemistry
- No practical applications due to rarity and radioactivity

Fun Fact

Lawrencium is the final member of the actinide series, completing the f‑block of the periodic table.
