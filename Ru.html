Ru
Ruthenium
Atomic Number: 44

Atomic Mass: 101.07

Electronegativity: 2.2

Density: 12.37 g/cm³

Melting Point: 2607 K

Boiling Point: 4423 K

Electron Config: [Kr] 4d⁷ 5s¹

Discovery

Ruthenium was discovered in 1844 by Karl Ernst Claus in Russia.

Isotopes

Ruthenium has seven stable isotopes: ⁹⁶Ru, ⁹⁸Ru, ⁹⁹Ru, ¹⁰⁰Ru, ¹⁰¹Ru, ¹⁰²Ru, and ¹⁰⁴Ru.

Uses

- Electrical contacts and resistors
- Catalysts in chemical reactions
- Jewelry and alloys
- Solar cells and electronics

Fun Fact

Ruthenium is named after Ruthenia, the Latin word for Russia, where it was discovered.
