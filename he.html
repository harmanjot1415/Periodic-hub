He
Helium
Atomic Number: 2

Atomic Mass: 4.0026

Electronegativity: — (not applicable)

Density: 0.1786 g/L

Melting Point: ~0.95 K (under pressure)

Boiling Point: 4.22 K

Electron Config: 1s²

Discovery

Helium was first detected in the solar spectrum by Jules Janssen and Norman Lockyer in 1868.

Isotopes

Helium has two stable isotopes: Helium-3 (³He) and Helium-4 (⁴He).

Uses

- Cooling superconducting magnets (like in MRI machines)
- Filling balloons and airships
- Shielding gas in welding
- Cryogenics and scientific research

Fun Fact

Helium is the second most abundant element in the universe, but it’s very rare on Earth!
