Pa
Protactinium
Atomic Number: 91

Atomic Mass: 231.04

Electronegativity: 1.5

Density: 15.37 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1841 K (1568 °C)

Boiling Point: 4300 K (4027 °C)

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f2 6d1 7s2

Discovery

Protactinium was first identified in 1913 by Kazimierz Fajans and Oswald Helmuth Göhring, and later isolated in 1917 by Otto Hahn and Lise Meitner. Its name means "parent of actinium".

Isotopes

Protactinium has no stable isotopes. The most common is Pa‑231, with a half‑life of 32,760 years.

Uses

- Research in nuclear chemistry
- Tracer studies in geology
- Limited applications due to scarcity and radioactivity

Fun Fact

Protactinium is one of the rarest naturally occurring elements — only trace amounts exist in uranium ores.
