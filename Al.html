Al
Aluminium
Atomic Number: 13

Atomic Mass: 26.981538

Electronegativity: 1.61

Density: 2.70 g/cm³

Melting Point: 933.47 K (660.32 °C)

Boiling Point: 2792 K (2519 °C)

Electron Config: [Ne] 3s² 3p¹

Discovery

Aluminium was first isolated in 1825 by Hans Christian Ørsted, and later refined by Friedrich Wöhler.

Isotopes

Aluminium has one stable isotope: Aluminium-27 (²⁷Al). Several radioactive isotopes are also known.

Uses

- Lightweight construction materials (aircraft, cars, buildings)
- Packaging (aluminium foil, cans)
- Electrical transmission lines
- Alloys with copper, magnesium, and silicon

Fun Fact

Aluminium is the most abundant metal in Earth’s crust, making up about 8% by weight.
