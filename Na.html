Na
Sodium
Atomic Number: 11

Atomic Mass: 22.989769

Electronegativity: 0.93

Density: 0.968 g/cm³

Melting Point: 370.87 K (97.72 °C)

Boiling Point: 1156 K (882.9 °C)

Electron Config: [Ne] 3s¹

Discovery

Sodium was first isolated by Sir Humphry Davy in 1807 using electrolysis of molten sodium hydroxide.

Isotopes

Sodium has one stable isotope: Sodium-23 (²³Na). Several radioactive isotopes are also known.

Uses

- Table salt (sodium chloride)
- Street lighting (sodium vapor lamps)
- Heat transfer in nuclear reactors
- Chemical industry (production of sodium compounds)

Fun Fact

Sodium reacts violently with water, producing hydrogen gas and heat that can ignite the hydrogen!
