Rhenium
Atomic Number: 75

Atomic Mass: 186.207

Electronegativity: 1.9

Density: 21.02 g/cm³

Melting Point: 3459 K

Boiling Point: 5869 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f14 5d5 6s2

Discovery

Rhenium was discovered in 1925 by Ida Noddack, Walter Noddack, and Otto Berg. It was the last stable element to be discovered.

Isotopes

Rhenium has two naturally occurring isotopes: Re‑185 (stable) and Re‑187 (radioactive with a very long half‑life).

Uses

- Superalloys for jet engines
- Catalysts in petroleum refining
- Electrical contacts
- Thermocouples

Fun Fact

Rhenium has one of the highest melting points of all elements, second only to tungsten.
