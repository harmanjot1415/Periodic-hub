Scandium
Atomic Number: 21

Atomic Mass: 44.955

Electronegativity: 1.36

Density: 2.985 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1814 K (1541 °C)

Boiling Point: 3109 K (2836 °C)

Electron Config: [Ar] 3d¹ 4s²

Discovery

Scandium was discovered in 1879 by Lars Fredrik Nilson in minerals euxenite and gadolinite.

Isotopes

Scandium has one stable isotope: Scandium-45 (⁴⁵Sc). Several radioactive isotopes are also known.

Uses

- Aerospace components (lightweight alloys)
- Sports equipment (bicycles, baseball bats)
- High-intensity lamps
- Research in materials science

Fun Fact

Scandium is classified as a rare earth element, though it is more abundant than silver.
