Categories of Elements in the Periodic Table

1. Alkali Metals (Group 1)

Elements: Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr

Why they are called "Alkali"?

  • They form strong bases (alkalis) when reacting with water.
  • Very reactive metals.
  • Have 1 valence electron.

2. Alkaline Earth Metals (Group 2)

Elements: Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Ra

Why "Alkaline Earth"?

  • They form alkaline (basic) oxides.
  • Found in earth minerals.
  • Have 2 valence electrons.

3. Transition Metals (Groups 3–12)

Examples: Fe, Cu, Zn, Ag, Ni

Why “Transition Metals”?

  • They create a transition between reactive metals and non-metals.
  • Show multiple oxidation states.
  • Form coloured compounds.

4. Halogens (Group 17)

Elements: F, Cl, Br, I, At

Why “Halogens”?

  • “Halo” = salt, “gen” = producer → They form salts.
  • Highly reactive non-metals.
  • Have 7 valence electrons.

5. Noble Gases (Group 18)

Elements: He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe

Why “Noble Gases”?

  • Very unreactive (inert).
  • Full outer electron shell.
  • Exist freely as gases.

6. Lanthanides

Elements: La to Lu

Why “Lanthanides”?

  • Named after the first element, Lanthanum.
  • Rare-earth metals.
  • Known for magnetic and optical properties.

7. Actinides

Elements: Ac to Lr

Why “Actinides”?

  • Named after Actinium.
  • Most are radioactive.
  • Include Uranium and Thorium.

8. Metalloids

Examples: B, Si, Ge, As, Sb

Why “Metalloids”?

  • Have both metallic and non-metallic properties.
  • Used in electronics and semiconductors.

9. Non-Metals

Examples: C, N, O, P, S

Why “Non-Metals”?

  • Lack metallic properties.
  • Poor conductors.
  • Essential for life processes.
