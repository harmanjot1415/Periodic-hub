Os
Osmium
Atomic Number: 76

Atomic Mass: 190.23

Electronegativity: 2.2

Density: 22.59 g/cm³

Melting Point: 3306 K

Boiling Point: 5285 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f14 5d6 6s2

Discovery

Osmium was discovered in 1803 by Smithson Tennant. Its name comes from the Greek word "osme", meaning smell, due to the pungent odor of its oxide.

Isotopes

Osmium has several stable isotopes: Os‑184, Os‑187, Os‑188, Os‑189, Os‑190, and Os‑192.

Uses

- Fountain pen tips
- Electrical contacts
- Alloys for extreme durability
- Catalysts in chemical reactions

Fun Fact

Osmium is the densest naturally occurring element, even denser than lead and platinum!
