Xe
Xenon
Atomic Number: 54

Atomic Mass: 131.293

Electronegativity: 2.6

Density: 5.9 g/L (gas at STP)

Melting Point: 161.4 K

Boiling Point: 165.1 K

Electron Config: [Kr] 4d¹⁰ 5s² 5p⁶

Discovery

Xenon was discovered in 1898 by William Ramsay and Morris Travers in England.

Isotopes

Xenon has nine stable isotopes, including ¹²⁴Xe, ¹²⁶Xe, ¹²⁸Xe, ¹²⁹Xe, ¹³⁰Xe, ¹³¹Xe, ¹³²Xe, ¹³⁴Xe, and ¹³⁶Xe.

Uses

- Lighting (xenon lamps)
- Anesthetic in medicine
- Ion propulsion for spacecraft
- Imaging and research applications

Fun Fact

Xenon is one of the few noble gases that forms compounds, such as xenon hexafluoroplatinate.
