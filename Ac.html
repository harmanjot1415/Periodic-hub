Ac
Actinium
Atomic Number: 89

Atomic Mass: 227

Electronegativity: 1.1

Density: 10.07 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1323 K (1050 °C)

Boiling Point: 3473 K (3200 °C)

Electron Config: [Rn] 6d1 7s2

Discovery

Actinium was discovered in 1899 by Friedrich Oskar Giesel. Its name comes from the Greek word "aktinos", meaning ray or beam.

Isotopes

Actinium has no stable isotopes. The most common is Ac‑227, with a half‑life of about 21.8 years.

Uses

- Radiation therapy (experimental)
- Neutron sources
- Research in nuclear chemistry
- Limited applications due to radioactivity

Fun Fact

Actinium was the first non‑primordial radioactive element discovered, paving the way for nuclear science.
