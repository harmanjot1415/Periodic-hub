Am
Americium
Atomic Number: 95

Atomic Mass: 243

Electronegativity: 1.3

Density: 12 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1449 K (1176 °C)

Boiling Point: 2880 K (2607 °C)

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f7 7s2

Discovery

Americium was discovered in 1944 by Glenn T. Seaborg and his team at the University of Chicago. It was named after the Americas.

Isotopes

Americium has no stable isotopes. The most important are Am‑241 (half‑life 432 years) and Am‑243.

Uses

- Smoke detectors (Am‑241)
- Industrial gauges
- Research in nuclear chemistry
- Potential use in nuclear batteries

Fun Fact

Americium is the only synthetic element that is found in household devices — smoke detectors!
