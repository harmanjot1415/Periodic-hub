Md
Mendelevium
Atomic Number: 101

Atomic Mass: 258

Electronegativity: 1.3

Density: ~10.3 g/cm³ (estimated)

Melting Point: 1100 K (827 °C, estimated)

Boiling Point: unknown (radioactive decay dominates)

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f13 7s2

Discovery

Mendelevium was discovered in 1955 by a team led by Albert Ghiorso at the University of California, Berkeley. It was named in honor of Dmitri Mendeleev, creator of the periodic table.

Isotopes

Mendelevium has no stable isotopes. The most notable is Md‑258, with a half‑life of about 51 days.

Uses

- Purely scientific research
- Studies of heavy element chemistry
- No practical applications due to rarity and radioactivity

Fun Fact

Mendelevium was the first element discovered one atom at a time, using particle accelerators.
