Rf
Rutherfordium
Atomic Number: 104

Atomic Mass: ~267 (most stable isotope)

Electronegativity: ~1.3 (estimated)

Density: ~23 g/cm³ (predicted)

Melting Point: unknown (predicted ~2400 K)

Boiling Point: unknown

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f14 6d2 7s2

Discovery

Rutherfordium was first synthesized in 1964 at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, Russia, and independently confirmed in 1969 at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. It was named in honor of Ernest Rutherford, the father of nuclear physics.

Isotopes

Rutherfordium has no stable isotopes. The most long‑lived is Rf‑267, with a half‑life of about 1.3 hours.

Uses

- Purely scientific research
- Studies of superheavy element chemistry
- No practical applications due to short half‑life

Fun Fact

Rutherfordium was part of a naming dispute between Russian and American scientists, eventually resolved by IUPAC in 1997.
