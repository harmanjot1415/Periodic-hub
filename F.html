F
Fluorine
Atomic Number: 9

Atomic Mass: 18.998

Electronegativity: 3.98 (highest of all elements)

Density: 1.696 g/L

Melting Point: 53.48 K (-219.67 °C)

Boiling Point: 85.03 K (-188.12 °C)

Electron Config: [He] 2s² 2p⁵

Discovery

Fluorine was first isolated in 1886 by Henri Moissan, who won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1906 for this achievement.

Isotopes

Fluorine has only one stable isotope: Fluorine-19 (¹⁹F).

Uses

- Toothpaste and water fluoridation (fluoride compounds)
- Production of Teflon (non-stick coatings)
- Uranium enrichment (UF₆)
- Refrigerants and coolants (fluorocarbons)

Fun Fact

Fluorine is the most reactive element in the periodic table and can form compounds with almost all other elements.
