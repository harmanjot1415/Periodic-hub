Li
Lithium
Atomic Number: 3

Atomic Mass: 6.94

Electronegativity: 0.98

Density: 0.534 g/cm³

Melting Point: 453.65 K (180.5 °C)

Boiling Point: 1603 K (1330 °C)

Electron Config: [He] 2s¹

Discovery

Lithium was discovered by Johan August Arfvedson in 1817 while analyzing petalite ore.

Isotopes

Lithium has two stable isotopes: Lithium-6 (⁶Li) and Lithium-7 (⁷Li).

Uses

- Rechargeable lithium-ion batteries
- Treatment for bipolar disorder (lithium salts)
- Heat-resistant glass and ceramics
- Lightweight alloys for aircraft

Fun Fact

Lithium is the lightest metal and can even float on water!
