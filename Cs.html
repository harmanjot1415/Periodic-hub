Cs
Cesium
Atomic Number: 55

Atomic Mass: 132.905

Electronegativity: 0.79

Density: 1.93 g/cm³

Melting Point: 301.7 K

Boiling Point: 944 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 6s¹

Discovery

Cesium was discovered in 1860 by Robert Bunsen and Gustav Kirchhoff in Germany.

Isotopes

Cesium has one stable isotope: ¹³³Cs. Radioactive isotopes like ¹³⁷Cs are used in industry and medicine.

Uses

- Atomic clocks (most accurate timekeeping)
- Oil drilling fluids
- Medical applications (radiotherapy)
- Research in quantum physics

Fun Fact

Cesium is so reactive it can explode on contact with water, releasing hydrogen gas and heat.
