V
Vanadium
Atomic Number: 23

Atomic Mass: 50.9415

Electronegativity: 1.63

Density: 6.11 g/cm³

Melting Point: 2183 K

Boiling Point: 3680 K

Electron Config: [Ar] 3d³ 4s²

Discovery

Vanadium was discovered by Andrés Manuel del Río in 1801 in Mexico, and later confirmed in 1830 by Nils Gabriel Sefström.

Isotopes

Vanadium has one stable isotope: ⁵¹V. It also has a naturally occurring radioactive isotope: ⁵⁰V.

Uses

- Strengthening steel alloys
- Aerospace materials
- Catalysts in chemical reactions
- Vanadium redox batteries for energy storage

Fun Fact

Vanadium is named after Vanadis, the Norse goddess of beauty, because of its colorful compounds.
