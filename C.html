C
Carbon
Atomic Number: 6

Atomic Mass: 12.011

Electronegativity: 2.55

Density: 2.267 g/cm³ (graphite)

Melting Point: ~3823 K (3550 °C, graphite)

Boiling Point: ~4300 K (4027 °C)

Electron Config: [He] 2s² 2p²

Discovery

Carbon has been known since antiquity in forms such as charcoal and diamond; it was recognized as an element in the late 18th century.

Isotopes

Carbon has two stable isotopes: Carbon-12 (¹²C) and Carbon-13 (¹³C), plus the radioactive Carbon-14 (¹⁴C) used in radiocarbon dating.

Uses

- Steel production (carbon as an alloying element)
- Graphite in pencils and lubricants
- Diamonds for cutting tools and jewelry
- Carbon fibers for strong, lightweight materials
- Radiocarbon dating in archaeology

Fun Fact

Carbon is the basis of all known life — organic chemistry is essentially the chemistry of carbon!
