N
Nitrogen
Atomic Number: 7

Atomic Mass: 14.007

Electronegativity: 3.04

Density: 1.2506 g/L

Melting Point: 63.15 K (-210 °C)

Boiling Point: 77.36 K (-196 °C)

Electron Config: [He] 2s² 2p³

Discovery

Nitrogen was discovered in 1772 by Daniel Rutherford, who identified it as a distinct component of air.

Isotopes

Nitrogen has two stable isotopes: Nitrogen-14 (¹⁴N) and Nitrogen-15 (¹⁵N).

Uses

- Fertilizer production (ammonia)
- Food preservation (liquid nitrogen)
- Inert atmosphere for chemical processes
- Explosives and propellants (nitrogen compounds)

Fun Fact

Nitrogen makes up about 78% of Earth’s atmosphere, yet humans cannot breathe it directly!
