Br
Bromine
Atomic Number: 35

Atomic Mass: 79.904

Electronegativity: 2.96

Density: 3.12 g/cm³

Melting Point: 266 K

Boiling Point: 332 K

Electron Config: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s² 4p⁵

Discovery

Bromine was discovered in 1826 by Antoine Jérôme Balard in France.

Isotopes

Bromine has two stable isotopes: ⁷⁹Br and ⁸¹Br.

Uses

- Flame retardants
- Water treatment (bromine compounds)
- Pharmaceuticals
- Photography chemicals (silver bromide)

Fun Fact

Bromine is the only nonmetal element that is liquid at room temperature.
