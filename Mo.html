Mo
Molybdenum
Atomic Number: 42

Atomic Mass: 95.95

Electronegativity: 2.16

Density: 10.28 g/cm³

Melting Point: 2896 K

Boiling Point: 4912 K

Electron Config: [Kr] 4d⁵ 5s¹

Discovery

Molybdenum was discovered in 1778 by Carl Wilhelm Scheele, and isolated in 1781 by Peter Jacob Hjelm.

Isotopes

Molybdenum has seven stable isotopes, including ⁹²Mo, ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo, ⁹⁶Mo, ⁹⁷Mo, ⁹⁸Mo, and ¹⁰⁰Mo.

Uses

- Strengthening steel alloys
- Catalysts in petroleum refining
- Electrical contacts and filaments
- Essential trace element in biology

Fun Fact

Molybdenum has one of the highest melting points of all elements, making it ideal for high-temperature applications.
