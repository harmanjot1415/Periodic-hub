Ti
Titanium
Atomic Number: 22

Atomic Mass: 47.867

Electronegativity: 1.54

Density: 4.506 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1941 K (1668 °C)

Boiling Point: 3560 K (3287 °C)

Electron Config: [Ar] 3d² 4s²

Discovery

Titanium was discovered in 1791 by William Gregor in Cornwall, England, and later named by Martin Heinrich Klaproth.

Isotopes

Titanium has five stable isotopes: Titanium-46 (⁴⁶Ti), Titanium-47 (⁴⁷Ti), Titanium-48 (⁴⁸Ti), Titanium-49 (⁴⁹Ti), and Titanium-50 (⁵⁰Ti).

Uses

- Aerospace components (aircraft, spacecraft)
- Medical implants (biocompatible)
- Pigments (titanium dioxide in paints)
- Sports equipment and jewelry
- Strong, corrosion-resistant alloys

Fun Fact

Titanium is as strong as steel but 45% lighter, making it ideal for aerospace and medical applications.
