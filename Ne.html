Ne
Neon
Atomic Number: 10

Atomic Mass: 20.1797

Electronegativity: — (not applicable)

Density: 0.9002 g/L

Melting Point: 24.56 K (-248.59 °C)

Boiling Point: 27.07 K (-246.08 °C)

Electron Config: [He] 2s² 2p⁶

Discovery

Neon was discovered in 1898 by Sir William Ramsay and Morris Travers in London.

Isotopes

Neon has three stable isotopes: Neon-20 (²⁰Ne), Neon-21 (²¹Ne), and Neon-22 (²²Ne).

Uses

- Neon signs and advertising lights
- High-voltage indicators and vacuum tubes
- Cryogenics (as a refrigerant)
- Lasers and plasma studies

Fun Fact

Neon produces a bright red-orange glow when electrified, making it iconic in neon signs worldwide.
