Ta
Tantalum
Atomic Number: 73

Atomic Mass: 180.947

Electronegativity: 1.5

Density: 16.69 g/cm³

Melting Point: 3290 K

Boiling Point: 5731 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f14 5d3 6s2

Discovery

Tantalum was discovered in 1802 by Anders Gustaf Ekeberg. It is named after King Tantalus from Greek mythology.

Isotopes

Tantalum has two isotopes: Ta‑180m (a rare long‑lived metastable isotope) and Ta‑181 (stable).

Uses

- Capacitors in electronics
- Surgical instruments
- Superalloys for turbines
- Chemical equipment resistant to corrosion

Fun Fact

Tantalum is highly resistant to corrosion and does not absorb acid, making it extremely durable.
