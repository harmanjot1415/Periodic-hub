Fr
Francium
Atomic Number: 87

Atomic Mass: ~223

Electronegativity: ~0.7

Density: ~1.87 g/cm³ (estimated)

Melting Point: ~300 K (27 °C, estimated)

Boiling Point: ~950 K (677 °C, estimated)

Electron Config: [Rn] 7s1

Discovery

Francium was discovered in 1939 by Marguerite Perey at the Curie Institute in Paris. It was named after France.

Isotopes

Francium has no stable isotopes. The most long‑lived is Fr‑223, with a half‑life of about 22 minutes.

Uses

- No practical uses due to extreme rarity and radioactivity
- Research in nuclear physics
- Studied for atomic structure experiments

Fun Fact

Francium is so rare that only a few grams exist naturally on Earth at any given time!
