Erbium
Atomic Number: 68

Atomic Mass: 167.259

Electronegativity: 1.24

Density: 9.07 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1802 K

Boiling Point: 3141 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f¹² 6s²

Discovery

Erbium was discovered in 1843 by Carl Gustaf Mosander in Sweden.

Isotopes

Erbium has six stable isotopes, including ¹⁶²Er, ¹⁶⁴Er, ¹⁶⁶Er, ¹⁶⁷Er, ¹⁶⁸Er, and ¹⁷⁰Er.

Uses

- Fiber-optic communication (Er-doped amplifiers)
- Lasers
- Nuclear technology
- Glass coloring (pink hue)

Fun Fact

Erbium gives glass and ceramics a delicate pink color, often used in sunglasses and jewelry.
