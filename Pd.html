Pd
Palladium
Atomic Number: 46

Atomic Mass: 106.42

Electronegativity: 2.20

Density: 12.02 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1828 K

Boiling Point: 3236 K

Electron Config: [Kr] 4d¹⁰

Discovery

Palladium was discovered in 1803 by William Hyde Wollaston in England.

Isotopes

Palladium has six stable isotopes: ¹⁰²Pd, ¹⁰⁴Pd, ¹⁰⁵Pd, ¹⁰⁶Pd, ¹⁰⁸Pd, and ¹¹⁰Pd.

Uses

- Catalytic converters in cars
- Electronics and hydrogen purification
- Jewelry and dentistry
- Fuel cells

Fun Fact

Palladium can absorb up to 900 times its own volume of hydrogen.
