Tc
Technetium
Atomic Number: 43

Atomic Mass: 98 (most stable isotope)

Electronegativity: 1.9

Density: 11.5 g/cm³

Melting Point: 2430 K

Boiling Point: 4538 K

Electron Config: [Kr] 4d⁵ 5s²

Discovery

Technetium was discovered in 1937 by Carlo Perrier and Emilio Segrè in Italy.

Isotopes

Technetium has no stable isotopes. The most common is ⁹⁹mTc, widely used in medical imaging.

Uses

- Medical diagnostics (radioisotope ⁹⁹mTc)
- Corrosion inhibitors in steel
- Research in nuclear medicine

Fun Fact

Technetium’s name comes from the Greek word “technetos”, meaning “artificial,” since it was the first man-made element.
