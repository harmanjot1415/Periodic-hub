P
Phosphorus
Atomic Number: 15

Atomic Mass: 30.974

Electronegativity: 2.19

Density: 1.82 g/cm³ (white phosphorus)

Melting Point: 317.3 K (44.15 °C, white phosphorus)

Boiling Point: 553.7 K (280.5 °C)

Electron Config: [Ne] 3s² 3p³

Discovery

Phosphorus was discovered in 1669 by Hennig Brand, who isolated it from urine while searching for the philosopher’s stone.

Isotopes

Phosphorus has one stable isotope: Phosphorus-31 (³¹P). Several radioactive isotopes are also known.

Uses

- Fertilizers (phosphates)
- Matches and fireworks
- Detergents and cleaning agents
- Steel production and metallurgy
- DNA and biological molecules (essential element for life)

Fun Fact

Phosphorus glows in the dark (chemiluminescence) when exposed to oxygen, giving it the name “light-bearer”.
