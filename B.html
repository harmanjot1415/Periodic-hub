B
Boron
Atomic Number: 5

Atomic Mass: 10.81

Electronegativity: 2.04

Density: 2.34 g/cm³

Melting Point: 2349 K (2076 °C)

Boiling Point: 4200 K (3927 °C)

Electron Config: [He] 2s² 2p¹

Discovery

Boron was first isolated in 1808 by Sir Humphry Davy, Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, and Louis Jacques Thénard.

Isotopes

Boron has two stable isotopes: Boron-10 (¹⁰B) and Boron-11 (¹¹B).

Uses

- Borosilicate glass (heat-resistant)
- Detergents and bleaches (borax)
- Semiconductors (as a dopant)
- Neutron absorber in nuclear reactors

Fun Fact

Boron is essential for plant growth and is a key micronutrient in agriculture.
