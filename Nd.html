Nd
Neodymium
Atomic Number: 60

Atomic Mass: 144.242

Electronegativity: 1.14

Density: 7.01 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1297 K

Boiling Point: 3347 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f⁴ 6s²

Discovery

Neodymium was discovered in 1885 by Carl Auer von Welsbach in Austria.

Isotopes

Neodymium has seven stable isotopes, including ¹⁴²Nd, ¹⁴³Nd, ¹⁴⁵Nd, ¹⁴⁶Nd, ¹⁴⁸Nd, and ¹⁵⁰Nd.

Uses

- Powerful permanent magnets (NdFeB)
- Lasers
- Glass coloring (purple)
- Headphones and microphones

Fun Fact

Neodymium magnets are among the strongest permanent magnets known, used in modern electronics.
