Pr
Praseodymium
Atomic Number: 59

Atomic Mass: 140.907

Electronegativity: 1.13

Density: 6.77 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1208 K

Boiling Point: 3793 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f³ 6s²

Discovery

Praseodymium was discovered in 1885 by Carl Auer von Welsbach in Austria.

Isotopes

Praseodymium has one stable isotope: ¹⁴¹Pr.

Uses

- High-strength alloys for aircraft engines
- Permanent magnets
- Glass coloring (yellow-green)
- Carbon arc lamps for studio lighting

Fun Fact

Praseodymium gives glass and ceramics a brilliant yellow-green color, used in safety goggles.
