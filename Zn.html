Zn
Zinc
Atomic Number: 30

Atomic Mass: 65.38

Electronegativity: 1.65

Density: 7.14 g/cm³

Melting Point: 692 K

Boiling Point: 1180 K

Electron Config: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s²

Discovery

Zinc has been used since ancient times, but was first isolated in 1746 by Andreas Marggraf.

Isotopes

Zinc has five stable isotopes: ⁶⁴Zn, ⁶⁶Zn, ⁶⁷Zn, ⁶⁸Zn, and ⁷⁰Zn.

Uses

- Galvanization (protecting steel from corrosion)
- Alloys such as brass
- Nutritional supplements (essential trace element)
- Batteries (zinc-carbon, zinc-air)

Fun Fact

Zinc is vital for the human immune system — deficiency can lead to health problems.
