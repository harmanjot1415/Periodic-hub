Si
Silicon
Atomic Number: 14

Atomic Mass: 28.085

Electronegativity: 1.90

Density: 2.33 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1687 K (1414 °C)

Boiling Point: 3538 K (3265 °C)

Electron Config: [Ne] 3s² 3p²

Discovery

Silicon was first identified in 1824 by Jöns Jacob Berzelius, who prepared it in pure form.

Isotopes

Silicon has three stable isotopes: Silicon-28 (²⁸Si), Silicon-29 (²⁹Si), and Silicon-30 (³⁰Si).

Uses

- Semiconductors and computer chips
- Solar panels
- Glass and ceramics
- Construction materials (concrete, bricks)
- Silicones in medical and industrial applications

Fun Fact

Silicon is the second most abundant element in Earth’s crust, making up about 27% by weight.
