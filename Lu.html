Lu
Lutetium
Atomic Number: 71

Atomic Mass: 174.9668

Electronegativity: 1.27

Density: 9.84 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1925 K

Boiling Point: 3675 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f14 5d1 6s2

Discovery

Lutetium was discovered in 1907 independently by Georges Urbain and Carl Auer von Welsbach. It is named after Lutetia, the ancient name for Paris.

Isotopes

Lutetium has one stable isotope: Lu‑175. It also has a long‑lived radioactive isotope Lu‑176.

Uses

- Used in catalysts for petroleum refining
- Medical imaging (PET scans)
- Research in nuclear physics
- LED light phosphors

Fun Fact

Lutetium is the last element in the lanthanide series and is sometimes grouped with transition metals.
