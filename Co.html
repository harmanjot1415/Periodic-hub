Co
Cobalt
Atomic Number: 27

Atomic Mass: 58.933

Electronegativity: 1.88

Density: 8.90 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1768 K

Boiling Point: 3200 K

Electron Config: [Ar] 3d⁷ 4s²

Discovery

Cobalt was discovered in 1735 by Georg Brandt, who proved it was the source of blue pigments.

Isotopes

Cobalt has one stable isotope: ⁵⁹Co. It also has several radioactive isotopes, including ⁶⁰Co, which is used in medicine and industry.

Uses

- Alloys for aircraft engines and turbines
- Rechargeable batteries (lithium-ion)
- Pigments for ceramics and glass
- Radiation source in cancer treatment (⁶⁰Co)

Fun Fact

The name “Cobalt” comes from the German word “Kobold”, meaning goblin, because miners thought cobalt ores were cursed.
