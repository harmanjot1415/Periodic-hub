O
Oxygen
Atomic Number: 8

Atomic Mass: 15.999

Electronegativity: 3.44

Density: 1.429 g/L

Melting Point: 54.36 K (-218.8 °C)

Boiling Point: 90.20 K (-182.9 °C)

Electron Config: [He] 2s² 2p⁴

Discovery

Oxygen was discovered independently by Carl Wilhelm Scheele in 1772 and Joseph Priestley in 1774.

Isotopes

Oxygen has three stable isotopes: Oxygen-16 (¹⁶O), Oxygen-17 (¹⁷O), and Oxygen-18 (¹⁸O).

Uses

- Medical oxygen for breathing support
- Steelmaking and welding
- Rocket oxidizer in propellants
- Water treatment and chemical synthesis

Fun Fact

Oxygen makes up about 21% of Earth’s atmosphere and is essential for life as we know it.
