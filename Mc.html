Mc
Moscovium
Atomic Number: 115

Atomic Mass: ~290 (most stable isotope)

Electronegativity: unknown (predicted ~1.3)

Density: ~13.5 g/cm³ (predicted)

Melting Point: unknown

Boiling Point: unknown

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f14 6d10 7s2 7p3

Discovery

Moscovium was first synthesized in 2003 by scientists at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, Russia, in collaboration with the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory in the USA. It was named after Moscow Oblast.

Isotopes

Moscovium has no stable isotopes. The most long‑lived is Mc‑290, with a half‑life of about 0.65 seconds.

Uses

- Purely scientific research
- Studies of superheavy element chemistry
- No practical applications due to extremely short half‑life

Fun Fact

Moscovium is predicted to behave like bismuth, its lighter homologue, but relativistic effects may alter its chemistry.
