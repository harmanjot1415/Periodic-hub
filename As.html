As
Arsenic
Atomic Number: 33

Atomic Mass: 74.922

Electronegativity: 2.18

Density: 5.72 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1090 K (sublimes instead of melting under normal pressure)

Boiling Point: 887 K

Electron Config: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s² 4p³

Discovery

Arsenic has been known since ancient times; it was first isolated by Albertus Magnus in 1250.

Isotopes

Arsenic has one stable isotope: ⁷⁵As. Several radioactive isotopes are also known.

Uses

- Semiconductors (gallium arsenide)
- Wood preservatives
- Herbicides and pesticides (historical)
- Alloys for strengthening metals

Fun Fact

Arsenic compounds were historically used as pigments, but many are highly toxic.
