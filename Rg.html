Rg
Roentgenium
Atomic Number: 111

Atomic Mass: ~282 (most stable isotope)

Electronegativity: unknown (predicted ~1.3)

Density: ~28–37 g/cm³ (predicted)

Melting Point: unknown

Boiling Point: unknown

Electron Config: [Rn] 5f14 6d9 7s2

Discovery

Roentgenium was first synthesized in 1994 by scientists at the Gesellschaft für Schwerionenforschung (GSI) in Darmstadt, Germany. It was named in honor of Wilhelm Röntgen, the discoverer of X‑rays.

Isotopes

Roentgenium has no stable isotopes. The most long‑lived is Rg‑282, with a half‑life of about 2.1 minutes.

Uses

- Purely scientific research
- Studies of superheavy element chemistry
- No practical applications due to short half‑life

Fun Fact

Roentgenium is predicted to behave like gold, its lighter homologue, in chemical reactions.
