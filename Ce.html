Ce
Cerium
Atomic Number: 58

Atomic Mass: 140.116

Electronegativity: 1.12

Density: 6.77 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1068 K

Boiling Point: 3716 K

Electron Config: [Xe] 4f¹ 5d¹ 6s²

Discovery

Cerium was discovered in 1803 by Jöns Jakob Berzelius and Wilhelm Hisinger in Sweden, and independently by Martin Heinrich Klaproth in Germany.

Isotopes

Cerium has four stable isotopes: ¹³⁶Ce, ¹³⁸Ce, ¹⁴⁰Ce, and ¹⁴²Ce.

Uses

- Catalysts in automotive exhaust systems
- Polishing glass and mirrors
- Alloys for lighter flints
- Nuclear industry applications

Fun Fact

Cerium is named after the dwarf planet Ceres, discovered just two years before the element.
